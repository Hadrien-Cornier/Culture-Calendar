# How this works

The Austin Culture Tipsheet is an opinionated weekly guide to films, concerts, opera, ballet, and literary events around Austin. It is assembled by an automated pipeline and then sorted by merit — not by chronology or venue marketing budget.

## Venues

Each week the pipeline scrapes or loads schedules from:

- Austin Film Society
- Hyperreal Film Club
- Paramount Theatre
- Austin Symphony
- Early Music Austin
- La Follia Austin Baroque
- Austin Opera
- Ballet Austin
- Alienated Majesty Books
- First Light Austin
- Arts on Alexander

Venues are added by hand. If a venue is missing, it is because no one has written a scraper for it yet.

## Ratings

Every event carries a single score on a 0–10 scale. Ratings are generated by a language model under the direction of a critic-style prompt and are intended to reflect three things in roughly this order:

1. **Artistic merit** — craft, performance quality, directorial voice, compositional depth.
2. **Originality** — how unusual the work is relative to what is typically on offer in Austin this month.
3. **Cultural significance** — durability, canonical weight, relevance to live conversations in the arts.

A rating of 7 or above is the threshold for "worth your evening"; 8+ enters the weekly Top 10. Ratings are stable across reruns for the same event unless upstream data changes.

## Reviews

Reviews are written by Anthropic's Claude and Perplexity's Sonar working in sequence: Claude drafts the critical voice and the one-line hook, Sonar supplies factual scaffolding (cast, director, release year, composer dates, premiere context) sourced from Wikipedia, Letterboxd, and publisher metadata for the pilot event set. The tone aims for a working critic's voice — declarative, specific, unafraid of a verdict — rather than press-release prose.

## Mailing list

A weekly digest lands in your inbox every Monday morning with the Top 10 picks for the week ahead. One email per week — no daily blasts, no transactional noise, no cross-promotion. The list is operated on Buttondown's free tier; we store only the email address you provide.

Every issue carries a one-click unsubscribe link in the footer, and you can opt out at any time by visiting the unsubscribe page linked from `/archive.html`. Unsubscribing is immediate and removes your address from the list without follow-up email. The signup form on the masthead is the only place we collect addresses, and we do not share or sell them.

## Limitations

A few things to keep in mind:

- **Refusal edge cases.** Occasionally the model refuses to review an event (too little metadata, a name collision, a safety filter trip). The pipeline now filters those out at the description level, but earlier snapshots of the site may still show them.
- **Review churn.** Re-running the pipeline can produce a subtly different review for the same event. The rating is much more stable than the prose.
- **No human editorial step.** Nothing here has been fact-checked by a human before publication. Treat factual claims (dates, credits, lineages) as a starting point, not a citation.

If something looks wrong, it probably is. Corrections welcome.
